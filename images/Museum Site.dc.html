About the museum

We collect the paper on which trust was drawn.

Founded in 1934 in the aftermath of the Crash, The Ledger is the only museum devoted entirely to the flat documents of finance. Our holdings span a Florentine ledger leaf of 1470 to the last engraved certificates of the twentieth century.

We conserve these objects, study them as material culture, and make them legible — because how money was written down is a history of how people agreed to believe one another.
